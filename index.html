Sunlight sneaks past the blinds, casting a soft glow upon the small room you call home. Half awake, you feel the warmth of the bed and the soothing familiarity of your surroundings, and the frigid air outside beckons to you, locked outside by walls of brick and concrete.
The fragmented memories of last night’s dream fade out of your mind as concentration and focus take their place. A shame, it was a good dream, but even though the details are lost, a vague sense of happiness remains. “At least it was a happy dream,” you think to yourself as you get ready for class. You’ll even have time to indulge in breakfast today.
While you enjoy all of them, you only have one bottle. What will you have this morning?
The sunlight proves a bit of comfort against the cold, biting at the edges of your ears and the tips of your fingers. With your bottle full and your bag packed, you head out the front door, greeted by the cold, refreshing air.
You find yourself reaching for your headphones. “Maybe I should listen to something.”
The lecture hall is not precisely where you want to be right now, but work must be done and in due time, your diligence will surely pay off. You claim the seat you often sit in, not so far that you’ll spend all of lecture unfocused, but not so close that you can hear the instructor’s breathing.
You grasp onto the fragments of conversation dancing around the room, find mild entertainment in catching brief whiffs of campus happenings, listen to everyone and everything as all individuals merge into one singular voice.
Perhaps you are not the best notetaker, but at least it will keep you focused in class, so you reach into your bag.
Everyone files out of the room in the usual ordered chaos, your friends in a hurry to catch their next class.
You, however, find yourself surrounded by gothic buildings under a pale blue sky, with an hour or so until the next class section, and the ever so sweet prospect of choice and freedom.
Not that you’ll spend it doing much of anything meaningful, of course.
You've been sitting around bored, lecture crawling along as you count the seconds away.
Yet as you walk out of the lecture hall you realize that time slipped by faster than you expect, reflected by the wispy clouds that have made their way into the sky, and the vague feeling of hunger in your stomach.
Lunch seems to be in order.
The sun casts oblique rays of the gentle warmth down upon campus as you exit your last class of the afternoon.
The soft golden glow, the soothing fabric of your scarf, the long, dark shadows cast by campus oaks, the coldness of the crisp spring air, the raw feeling of simply existing, they surround you, envelop you.
They surround and envelop the tired students running more on caffeine than on sleep, the cheerful students partying alongside their close friends, the anxious students waiting to hear back regarding their interview, the new students two quarters fresh into college, the veteran students two months away from graduation.
These sensations which surround everyone and everything remind you, and remind us all, of the uniqueness of existing here, and of the community that we call home.
After having a brief moment of existential reflection, you realize that you cannot just stand in the center of the quad forever. It would be rather difficult to do much schoolwork just standing there in the cold. Or any further thinking, for that matter.

… you go back to studying …
In the midst of dwelling over a rather difficult question on your problem set that has been keeping you a little frustrated and a little too deep in thought, you heard your phone buzz, and is asking where you are, and what you’re doing.
You wrap up work for the day, feeling success in your small accomplishments. Through the window you see that the setting sun has since been replaced by the faint glow of a crescent moon on this beautiful cloudless night.

It is a haven for the weary soul to regain strength and rest today, in preparation for the journey of tomorrow.
It is a portal of sorts, that takes you forward, slowly but surely, into the days and weeks and months that await you.
How do you prepare yourself for such a journey?
The line between dream and reality blurs as the clutches of sleep slowly tighten their grasp.
“It’s been a good day,” you think to yourself, or perhaps say in your dream, you are not quite sure.
You see the smiles of your classmates, hear the laughs of your friends, feel the warmth of the sun yet the cold of the air.
